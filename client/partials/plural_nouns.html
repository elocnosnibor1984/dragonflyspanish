Plural Nouns

In English we generally make nouns plural by adding an "s" (example: chairs), but there are a lot of exceptions (foxes, fish, cacti, oxen, etc). In Spanish it is much simpler: when a noun ends in a vowel add "s" and when it ends in a consonant add "es".

Examples:

el hombre becomes los hombres

la mujer becomes las mujeres

(Currently "mujer" is the only noun we have ending in a consonant, but we'll get more soon!)
